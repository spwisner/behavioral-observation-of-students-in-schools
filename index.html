BOSS Web Application

Behavioral Observation of Students in Schools (BOSS) is a research-based systematic observation method. BOSS allows observers, such as school counselors, to be able to compare the behavior of a target student to that of his peers. This is beneficial in determining if a specific student’s behavior deviates from the rest of the class and if a problem exists.

Conducting BOSS

BOSS is conducted by categorizing behavior at a specific interval of time. It is easiest to explain this observation method using an example. Suppose a school counselor is asked to observe a particular student using BOSS. The observer enters the classroom and decides to make an observation every 15 seconds over 40 intervals (that is, a 10 minute session). For each interval, the observer selects at least one of the five options for behavior displayed by a student:

  1. Actively Engaged Time (AET) - e.g. answering a teacher’s question
  2. Passively Engaged Time (PET) - e.g. reading an assigned passage
  3. Off-Task Motor (OFT-M) - e.g. tapping a pen
  4. Off-Task Verbal (OFT-V) - e.g. talking to a friend
  5. Off-Task Passive (OFT-P) - e.g. staring out the window

Now, in order to be able to compare the target student to the rest of the class, data needs to be collected for random peers as well. For this reason, at every 5th interval, the observer directs his attention to a random student and categorizes his or her behavior during that interval.

At the end of the 10 minutes, the observer will have 40 data points of behavior. 32 of the data points reflect the behavior of the target student and 8 of the data points (which was collected every 5th interval) represent the behavior of random peers.

Benefits of the App

Conducting BOSS with a timer, pen, and paper can be challenging. It is difficult to monitor a student’s behavior while keeping track of time and marking down results. This web application attempts to simplify the data collection process by having observations automatically submitted at the end of a time interval. In addition, this application eliminates the tedious process of compiling results. At the end of a session, a statistical report is automatically generated.
